Gaura Style Size Guide

Find the fit that feels effortless

Our size guides for rings and bracelets are designed to help you measure at home with confidence. A good fit should feel secure, comfortable, and easy to wear every day.

If you are between sizes, we usually recommend choosing the larger size for a more natural fit.

Ring Size Guide

How to measure your ring size

Find your size in two ways: measure the inside diameter of a ring you already own, or the circumference of your finger. Open a method below, then match your measurement to the chart.

1 Option 1 Find your diameter

Choose a ring that fits the finger you plan to wear your new ring on, then measure the inside diameter.

  1. Place the ring on a ruler.
  2. Measure the inside edge from one side to the other.
  3. Use the inside diameter in millimeters to find your size in the chart.
2 Option 2 Find your circumference

If you do not already have a ring that fits, measure your finger directly.

  1. Wrap a thin strip of paper or soft measuring tape around the base of your finger.
  2. Make sure it feels snug but not tight.
  3. Mark the point where the strip overlaps, then measure the length in millimeters.
  4. Use that circumference measurement in the chart.
3 Then Match your measurement

Compare your diameter or circumference with the chart to find your fit. If your measurement falls between two rows, choose the larger size.

Ring fit tips

Getting it right

Measure more than once

Finger size can shift through the day with temperature, hydration, and activity. For the most reliable result, measure two or three times at different times of day.

Think about the knuckle

Your ring should slide on with ease and come off without forcing it. If your knuckle is wider than the base of your finger, choose a size that comfortably passes over it.

If you are between sizes

Choose the larger size, especially if you prefer a relaxed fit or plan to wear the ring for long hours.

For wider bands

Wide-band rings often fit more snugly than delicate bands. We recommend sizing up slightly for styles with a broader shank.

For stackable rings

If you plan to stack several rings together, going up half a size can create a more comfortable fit.

A quiet surprise

Buying a ring as a gift

Trying to keep it a surprise? Here are a few subtle ways to estimate the right size.

Check a ring they wear

Measure the inside diameter of a ring they already wear on the correct finger.

Ask someone close

A friend, sibling, or partner may already know their usual size.

Compare by fit

Try one of their rings on your own finger and note where it sits. A jeweler can help estimate the size from there.

Bracelet Size Guide

How to measure your bracelet size

Measure your wrist, then add a little room depending on how close or loose you want the fit. Open a step below, then match it to the chart.

1 Step 1 Measure your wrist

Wrap a soft measuring tape around your wrist just below the wrist bone.

  1. Keep the tape close to the skin without pulling tightly.
  2. Note your wrist measurement in centimeters or inches.
2 Step 2 Add room for comfort

Add a little extra length depending on how close or relaxed you want the fit.

Bracelet fit tips

Getting it right

If you are between sizes

Choose the next size up for a more comfortable fit.

Consider your style

A slim chain bracelet sits differently from a rigid bangle or a charm bracelet. Structured styles often need a little more room.

Leave space for charms

If you plan to add charms, select a slightly larger size so the bracelet still feels comfortable once styled.

Keep movement in mind

A bracelet should move naturally on the wrist without feeling restrictive or sliding too far down the hand.

By style

Choosing the right bracelet fit

For chain bracelets

A little movement creates the most effortless look. Aim for a fit that feels secure but not tight.

For bangles

Your hand needs to pass through comfortably. Size with the widest part of your hand in mind, not only your wrist.

For charm bracelets

Leave extra room for future styling. As charms are added, the bracelet will feel smaller.

For cuffs or open bracelets

These should sit neatly on the wrist without pinching. If you prefer a softer fit, size up.

Quick sizing advice

When in doubt

Choose rings that feel balanced

Your ring should stay in place while still sliding over the knuckle without strain.

Choose bracelets that feel easy

Your bracelet should rest naturally on the wrist and move with you, not against you.

When in doubt

If you are between sizes, the safer choice is usually the larger one.
