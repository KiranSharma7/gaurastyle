# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Women 25–45, browsing on mobile and desktop during leisure time. Comparing Gaura Style against established luxury jewelry brands (Cartier, Tiffany, Mejuri). Making a purchase decision without seeing the piece in person — the interface must earn trust immediately at every landing moment.

## Product Purpose

Gaura Style is a creative house whose product is **original jewelry design**. The pieces — primarily rings — are the medium; the design itself is what's being sold. The brand is not moving inventory, it is presenting work: every piece exists because the design exists. Sold direct-to-consumer across four markets: US, Japan, India, Russia.

The site's job is to convert desire into purchase by foregrounding the design at every moment — making the visitor feel like they've discovered a singular object, not selected from a catalog. A curated gallery, not a warehouse. Design must dominate every screen; price, CTA, and commerce mechanics recede until the eye has met the work.

## Brand Personality

Bold. Confident. Modern. Not whispering luxury — this brand makes a statement. Decisive and self-assured like a high-end fashion house that knows exactly who it is. Contemporary without trend-chasing.

Emotional goal: desire and aspiration. Every landing moment should make the visitor want to own the jewelry.

Emotional bar — every screen must clear all three: **Luxury** (does it feel expensive without trying?), **Premium** (is the craft evident in every detail?), **Wow** (is there at least one moment that stops the scroll?).

Voice: confident (not boastful), warm (not effusive), aspirational (not exclusive), direct second-person ("Find your ring"). No superlatives, no exclamation marks, no emoji.

## Anti-references

Generic WooCommerce product grids. Discount-retailer clutter. Dark-mode neon. Heavy animations and scroll theatrics. Cold sterile minimalism with no warmth. Whispering neutrality (the Tiffany blue-and-silence aesthetic). Anything that reads as "e-commerce template."

## Design Principles

1. **The design is the product.** What's for sale is the originality of the piece, not the material or the transaction. Every screen must showcase the design itself as prominently as possible — large imagery, generous space around each piece, multiple angles, room to study the work. Commerce affordances (price, add-to-cart, badges) appear only after the design has been seen, and never compete with it visually.
2. **Earn trust before asking for action.** The purchase decision happens without touch — every visual choice must communicate quality and craft before a CTA appears.
3. **Restraint as statement.** Say more by showing less. Restraint is not timidity; it's confidence that the work speaks for itself.
4. **Warmth is non-negotiable.** Cool tones, cold whites, and sterile grids contradict the brand. Every surface, shadow, and photograph must read warm.
5. **Bold, not loud.** Typography and scale carry authority; animation and decoration do not. Big, decisive type over ornamental flourishes.
6. **Curated gallery, not warehouse.** Every product moment should feel selected, not listed. Spacing, photography, and editorial rhythm over density.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Reduced-motion support for all transitions. Color contrast verified against `--bg` and `--surface-warm` backgrounds. No color as sole information carrier.
